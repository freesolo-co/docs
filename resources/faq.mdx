Whether you're evaluating Freesolo for your team or you're already building, the questions below cover the most common things customers ask before and after signing up. If your question isn't answered here, reach out directly at [founders@freesolo.co](mailto:founders@freesolo.co) — you'll hear back from a real engineer.

<Accordion title="What is Freesolo and who is it for?">
  Freesolo is an AI platform designed for enterprise teams that want to build, evaluate, train, and deploy product-native AI models and features. It's built for teams that have moved beyond experimenting with off-the-shelf AI and are ready to develop AI capabilities that are specific to their product, their data, and their users.

  Freesolo is operated by Linkd Inc. and is designed for engineering and product teams at companies where AI is a core part of the product — not an afterthought.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How is Freesolo different from just calling a frontier model API?">
  Calling a frontier model API gives you a general-purpose model. Freesolo gives you the infrastructure to make that model — or a model you train yourself — work for your specific use case.

  That means tooling for systematic evaluation so you know whether your AI actually performs well on your task, pipelines for fine-tuning on your own data so the model reflects your product's behavior, and deployment infrastructure so you can serve your model reliably in production. Freesolo handles the full lifecycle: build, evaluate, train, deploy — so your team doesn't have to stitch together a dozen separate tools.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What AI models does Freesolo work with?">
  Freesolo is designed to work across the AI model ecosystem rather than locking you into a single provider. Whether you're working with frontier models, open-weight models, or models you've trained yourself, Freesolo's evaluation, training, and deployment workflows are built to accommodate your stack.

  For specifics about model integrations relevant to your use case, [book a call](https://cal.com/freesolo/chat) with the team or email [founders@freesolo.co](mailto:founders@freesolo.co).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do I need to manage GPU infrastructure for training?">
  No. Freesolo manages the underlying compute infrastructure for training pipelines. You focus on your data, your model configuration, and your evaluation criteria — Freesolo handles provisioning, orchestration, and scaling the infrastructure needed to run your jobs.

  This is especially valuable for enterprise teams that don't want to build and maintain a separate MLOps stack just to fine-tune or train models.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long does a training pipeline take?">
  Training duration depends on the size of your dataset, the model you're training, the type of training (fine-tuning vs. training from a checkpoint), and the compute configuration used. Freesolo's infrastructure is designed to complete training jobs efficiently, and the engineering team can advise on expected timelines for your specific workload during onboarding.

  For time-sensitive production workflows, discuss your latency requirements with the team during your [initial call](https://cal.com/freesolo/chat).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Is my data used to train other customers' models?">
  No. Freesolo does not use one customer's private Customer Content to train or improve models for another customer.

  Your prompts, datasets, labels, evaluation traces, logs, and any other content you submit to Freesolo remain private to your account. The only exceptions are situations you explicitly authorize — for example, content you make public, or aggregated and fully de-identified data that cannot be attributed back to you. See the [Data Privacy](/security/data-privacy) page for a full breakdown.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What compliance certifications does Freesolo hold?">
  Freesolo holds the following certifications:

  - **ISO 27001** — Internationally recognized standard for information security management systems.
  - **SOC 2 Type I** — Confirms security controls are suitably designed as of an audit date.
  - **SOC 2 Type II** — Confirms security controls operated effectively over an extended audit period.

  Freesolo also undergoes regular third-party penetration tests and security reviews. Audit reports are available to enterprise customers under NDA — email [founders@freesolo.co](mailto:founders@freesolo.co) to request documentation. You can also read more on the [Security Overview](/security/overview) page.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What does '24/7 engineering support' mean in practice?">
  It means that when you have a critical issue, you reach Freesolo engineers directly — not a tiered support queue or a generalist support agent. Enterprise customers get a dedicated channel staffed by the people who built and operate the platform.

  24/7 coverage means that if something breaks in production at 2am on a Sunday, you can reach the team and get a substantive response — not an automated acknowledgment. For non-critical questions, you can also expect a response within one business day.
</Accordion>
