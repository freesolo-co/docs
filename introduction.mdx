Building an AI feature that works in a demo is straightforward. Building one that works reliably inside a real product, for real users, at scale — that's a different problem entirely. Freesolo is designed to solve exactly that: it gives enterprise teams a structured engine to develop, evaluate, and improve AI features using the context that actually matters — your workflows, your data, and your users' feedback.

## Who Freesolo is for

Freesolo is built for enterprise product and engineering teams who are moving AI features from proof-of-concept into production. If your team is dealing with inconsistent model outputs, unclear evaluation criteria, slow iteration cycles, or difficulty connecting model behavior to real product outcomes, Freesolo was designed with you in mind.

Whether you're embedding AI into an internal tool, a customer-facing workflow, or a core product surface, Freesolo gives you the infrastructure and expert support to do it with confidence.

## The Freesolo Engine

Every feature you build on Freesolo moves through a three-phase engine designed to create a continuous feedback loop between your product and your models.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Instrument">
    Freesolo captures how your AI feature behaves in context using real workflow data. Rather than relying on synthetic benchmarks or isolated test cases, Instrumentation grounds your feature in the actual conditions it will face — the inputs, the edge cases, and the usage patterns that emerge from your product.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Evaluate">
    Once your feature is instrumented, Freesolo measures its quality across four dimensions: output quality, latency, reliability, and cost. You define the eval criteria that matter to your use case, and Freesolo surfaces results in a way that makes trade-offs visible and actionable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Improve">
    Freesolo closes the loop by using live product feedback to continuously refine your feature. As real users interact with your AI, that signal feeds back into your improvement cycle — making your model smarter over time without requiring you to start from scratch.
  </Step>
</Steps>
