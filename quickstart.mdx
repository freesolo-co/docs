This guide walks you through everything you need to get your first AI feature off the ground on Freesolo. You'll create an account, set up your workspace, define a feature, and run an evaluation — all through Freesolo's UI. No code required to get started.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Navigate to [freesolo.co/sign-up](https://freesolo.co/sign-up) to create your Freesolo account. You have three sign-up options:

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Google SSO">
        Click **Continue with Google** and authenticate with your Google account. You'll be redirected back to Freesolo automatically once authentication is complete.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="GitHub SSO">
        Click **Continue with GitHub** and authorize the Freesolo application in GitHub. You'll be redirected back to Freesolo once the handshake is complete.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Email & Password">
        Enter your work email address and create a password of at least 8 characters. You'll receive a verification email — click the link inside to activate your account before signing in.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>

    <Note>
      Use your work email address when signing up. This makes it easier to associate your account with your organization and streamlines team invites later.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up your workspace">
    After signing in for the first time, Freesolo prompts you to set up your workspace.

    1. **Create an organization** — Enter your company or team name. This becomes the top-level container for all your projects and members.
    2. **Name your first project** — Projects are how Freesolo organizes AI features, datasets, and evals. Give this project a clear, descriptive name that reflects the product area or use case you're starting with.
    3. **Invite team members** — Enter the email addresses of your colleagues. Each invitee receives an email with a link to join your organization. You can assign roles (Admin, Member, or Viewer) at invite time and update them later from the team settings panel.

    <Tip>
      You can create additional projects at any time from the project switcher in the top navigation bar. One organization can contain multiple projects, so you don't need to create a new account for each use case.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Define your first AI feature">
    With your workspace ready, navigate to **Features** in the left sidebar and click **New Feature**.

    Fill in the following fields:

    - **Feature name** — A short, descriptive label for the capability you're building (e.g., "Contract Summarizer" or "Support Ticket Classifier").
    - **Description** — Explain what the feature does and what a good output looks like. This context is used by Freesolo during instrumentation and eval design.
    - **Constraints** — Specify any hard requirements such as maximum response length, restricted topics, tone, or output format. Constraints are enforced during evaluation.
    - **Workflow context** — Describe the product surface and user workflow this feature lives in. The more context you provide here, the more accurately Freesolo can instrument the feature with representative data.

    Click **Save Feature** when you're done. Your feature is now ready to be instrumented.

    <Info>
      You can return to edit your feature definition at any time. Updates to constraints and workflow context take effect on the next evaluation run.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run your first evaluation">
    Open your newly created feature and click **Run Evaluation**.

    1. **Select eval definitions** — Choose from the eval templates available in your project, or create a custom eval definition that reflects your feature's quality criteria. Evals measure output quality, latency, reliability, and cost.
    2. **Choose a model** — Select the model you want to evaluate from the model settings panel. You can compare multiple models in a single run.
    3. **Review the configuration** — Check your eval parameters, dataset selection, and concurrency settings, then click **Start Evaluation**.

    Freesolo runs the evaluation and displays results in the **Eval Results** dashboard. Each run shows a breakdown by dimension — quality scores, p50/p95 latency, error rates, and estimated cost per call — so you can make informed decisions about model selection and iteration priorities.

    <Warning>
      Evaluation runs consume credits based on the number of model calls and the models selected. Check your usage limits in the **Usage & Billing** section before running large-scale evals.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Book a call with the engineering team">
    Freesolo customers get direct access to the engineering team throughout their journey. Whether you're planning a training run, interpreting eval results, or preparing for a production deployment, the team is here to help.

    Book a 30-minute call at [cal.com/freesolo/chat](https://cal.com/freesolo/chat) to:

    - Walk through your eval results and identify the highest-impact improvements
    - Design a dataset curation or fine-tuning strategy tailored to your use case
    - Plan your deployment configuration and monitoring setup
    - Get answers to any technical questions about the Freesolo platform

    <Tip>
      We recommend booking an onboarding call within your first week. Teams that connect with the engineering team early ship production-ready features significantly faster.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
